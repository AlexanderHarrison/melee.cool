rwing

rwing is an advanced replay viewer for Super Smash Bros. Melee.

With rwing, you can easily browse and watch back your SLP replay files, review tournament vods for mistakes, study top-level gameplay vods, export missed combos as UnclePunch Training Mode savestates, share annoted vods with students, and much more!

Available for windows, linux, and macos!

Filtering Replays

Rwing can filter through thousands of slp files instantly. Filter by tag, slippi connect code, character, stage, and singles/teams.

You can also mark special games with a star to find them easier, and filter for games that you have annotated.

You can open the files filter anywhere with View→Files Filter.

UnclePunch's Training Mode Exports

Here's a guide on setting up TM Exports

You can export any moment in your games as a Training Mode savestate. This makes practicing your mistakes simple. You can start to fix any issue with your play immediately.

To export a situation, press the button in the game controls.

Then, you can open the savestate in Training Mode to practice.

Improover

Rwing can mass export moments in your games on a specified button press. Press dpad-down on your mistakes in-game, and then use the improover to export those moments.

Open the improover with Tools→Improover.

You can add games and mass export all button presses by character, connect code, or port.

Use Add Recent Games to only add newly played games.

Then select and preview your moments, and export them in batch!

Notes and Diagrams

You can write notes and draw diagrams for moments in your games. These will be saved into the slp file. This means that you can easily share these with students or teammates.

You can also draw diagrams showing bad movement or to visualize spacings. These are also saved in the slp!

To remove notes or diagrams, use Right Click→Remove.

Timeline

You can use the timeline to find and mark moments in your games. Stocks, notes, and diagrams will all be automatically marked on the timeline.

Theicon marks a dpad down input. This can be used as you like to mark any moment in a game. For example, you can use it to clip a cool combo, or mark something to practice.

Zoom into the timeline using the scroll wheel and pan around with right click.

Select a portion of the timeline using right click. Use the frame markers to modify the selection. Right click outside the bars to remove the selection.

You can create notes and diagrams over ranges of frames by adding notes or diagrams during a selection.

The icon zooms the timeline to the current selection.

Slp Compression

You can use rwing to compress slp files to 1/10th the size. Compressed files are 100% supported by rwing. You can still filter, watch back, and export TM savestates from compressed files.

Slp files are around 3Mb per replay. These files can pile up and take up many gigabytes of space. If you compress them, you can store 10x as many replay files!

To compress every replay file, use Tools→Compress Replays.

You can selectively compress/decompress a specific replay using its context menu.

Combo Finder

You can use rwing to find combos in your games. This is similar to the combo finder in Clippi, just simpler and less powerful. To open the combo finder, go to Tools→Find Combos.

You can re-order and remove clips as you see fit.

To render your clips in dolphin, export a playlist of your clips using Playlist→Save. Then open this playlist in dolphin like in Clippi.

You can open your Clippi playlists in rwing to easily modify the clips using Rwing→Open Playlist.

Recording Management

Managing your Training Mode recordings can be a little annoying. Rwing provides a means to delete, rename, and find your recordings. To open the recording manager, go to Tools→Manage Recordings.

You can easily delete recordings in batch.

And find recordings in your file manager to share with others.

Layouts

Rwing provides many useful panels that you can pop out and arrange to suit your workflow. Press Ctrl+Right Click on any panel to move it around.

You can also drag outside the window to popout the panel. Likewise, drag back in the main window to pop back in.

Layouts are saved between rwing launches. You can use View→Save Layout and Save→Open Layout to manually save and restore different layouts.

To restore the default layout, use View→Restore Layout.

OSDs and Overlays

You can adjust various overlays and information to show up on-screen. Open the overlay menu with the icon in the game controls.

Wavedash

The wavedash OSD will show if it was early or late, and whether it was a short or fullhop.

Frame Advantage

The frame advantage OSD will show how many frames you are actionable before the opponent after hitting the opponent's shield.

Actionable

Characters will glow a bright green when actionable.

Hitboxes

Use the icon in the game controls to toggle hitboxes.

Take Screenshots
